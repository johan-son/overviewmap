/04_frameworks

Third Place / Ray Oldenburg
Discussed in Oldenburg's book The Great Good Place, third places are locations separate from home (first place) and work (second place) that are intended to facilitate relaxation through community socialisation.

The internet is not a true "place", yet it has become our third place. Can the same principle be applied to fictionalised "places" that live on the internet?

Non-place / Marc Augé
Coined by anthropologist Marc Augv, a non-place refers to any location that is not intended for anyone to remain in; liminal locations that are merely the transition between two locations. Hallways, elevators, moving vehicles, are considered non-places.

Are there ways that the digital space can be transformed from a transient non-place into places where we live?
